If all you know about New Zealand wine is Marlborough sauvignon blanc, you're missing the real story. Yes, it's exceptional — and yes, it changed the global wine conversation in the 1980s. But the New Zealand wine scene in 2026 is vastly more diverse, more ambitious, and more interesting.

## Central Otago pinot noir

Central Otago is the world's southernmost wine region, and its pinot noir is extraordinary — fragrant, complex, and uniquely expressive of place. The Gibbston Valley, Bannockburn, and Cromwell Basin each produce wines with distinct characters, all within a short drive of Queenstown.

For pinot noir lovers from Sonoma or Willamette Valley, Central Otago is a revelation. The climate is continental — more extreme than either of those regions — and the wines reflect it: brighter acidity, more minerality, and a freshness that's hard to find elsewhere.

## Hawke's Bay syrah

Hawke's Bay, on the North Island's east coast, is producing syrah that rivals the northern Rhône. The Gimblett Gravels — a unique alluvial gravel terroir — gives these wines a warmth and complexity that's startling. Paired with the region's lamb and olive oil, it's a food-and-wine experience that stands alongside anything in the world.
