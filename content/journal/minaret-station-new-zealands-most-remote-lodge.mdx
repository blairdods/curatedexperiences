There are lodges that are hard to reach, and then there's Minaret Station.

The property sits in a remote valley above Lake Wanaka, surrounded by the peaks of the Southern Alps on three sides. There is no road. The only way in is helicopter — a 20-minute flight from Wanaka that crosses mountain ranges and descends into a valley that feels completely separated from the world below.

That journey in is part of the experience. By the time you land, something has shifted. The noise of everything else you've been carrying — itinerary, schedule, the sense of obligation to keep moving — has been left behind somewhere over the mountains.

## What the property is

Minaret Station is a working high-country merino station. The accommodation is in six private villas, each positioned to maximise the mountain view and minimise visibility of the others. The design is contemporary, with generous glazing to frame the landscape, and every comfort you'd expect from a property at this level. But it doesn't feel like a hotel. It feels like a place someone lives in and cares about.

The station has been in the same family for generations. That continuity matters — you feel it in the way the property is run, in the details of how the food is sourced and prepared, and in the knowledge of the guides who've grown up working this land.

## What you do

The activity menu at Minaret is outdoor and mountain-focused: guided heli-hiking in the surrounding ranges, fly-fishing on private waters, mountain biking, and — if the season and conditions allow — deer stalking on the station runs. In winter, the property has direct access to some of the finest off-piste skiing terrain in New Zealand, accessed by helicopter.

The fishing deserves a particular mention. The station has exclusive access to waters that no other operator works — high-altitude lakes and streams that hold wild brown trout in a condition that professional guides describe as unlike anything accessible to regular anglers. For fly-fishing guests, this alone justifies the stay.

## The food

Given that you're arriving by helicopter, the quality of the food at Minaret Station surprises most guests. The kitchen is serious. The menu changes with the seasons and with what the station and nearby farms are producing. Lamb raised on the property. Venison from the runs. The wine list has genuine depth, with a Central Otago focus that makes sense given the location — the vineyards of Wanaka and Bannockburn are 20 minutes away by air.

## How we position it in a journey

Minaret Station works best as a stand-alone experience of two or three nights rather than a one-night stop. The pace of the property — and the fact that the helicopter in and out are events in themselves — suits a longer stay.

We typically position it in the South Island section of a longer journey, between a Queenstown or Wanaka base and onward travel to the West Coast or Fiordland. It can also serve as the final property in a South Island journey — the last thing you experience before flying home, which creates a particular kind of ending.

For guests who haven't been to New Zealand before and are asking what the single most distinctive experience on the menu is, Minaret Station is one of our honest answers.
