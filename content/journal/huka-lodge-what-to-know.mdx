Huka Lodge opened in 1924 as a fishing camp on the banks of the Waikato River, a short walk above the Huka Falls. It has hosted the Queen, multiple US presidents, and more high-net-worth travellers than almost any property in the Southern Hemisphere. And yet it remains, despite everything, a place that doesn't feel like it's performing.

That's the thing about Huka that most descriptions don't quite capture. The reputation is extraordinary — which means guests arrive expecting to be impressed. And somehow the lodge sidesteps the pressure of that expectation by being, at its core, simply good. The kind of good that comes from 100 years of doing one thing and caring about it deeply.

## The setting

The Waikato River below Lake Taupō carries an extraordinary volume of water — the entire outflow of the country's largest lake — and it narrows through a basalt gorge just above Huka Falls before dropping 11 metres in a single sheet of turquoise. The sound is constant. The colour of the water, fed by the volcanic plateau that underlies this part of the North Island, is a shade of blue-green that seems almost engineered.

The lodge sits above the riverbank in a private garden, surrounded by podocarp trees and the kind of birdsong that makes you realise how quiet New Zealand is once you're out of the cities.

## The rooms

There are 20 suites in total, all different, all facing the river. The original lodge building houses the dining room, bar, and a sitting room that has barely changed in decades — it has the quality of a room that has been genuinely lived in, not staged. The river suites closest to the water are the best of the accommodation, but the standard varies less than you might expect for the price.

The Owner's Cottage is a separate two-bedroom property on the property with its own kitchen and butler service, available for guests who want complete privacy.

## The fishing

Huka Lodge's reputation was built on trout fishing, and the fishing is still extraordinary. The Tongariro River, a short drive south, is one of the finest wild trout fisheries in the world — large rainbow and brown trout in clear, fast water. The lodge's fishing guides are experienced and genuinely knowledgeable. If fly fishing is part of why you're coming to New Zealand, Huka Lodge is the right base, and the Tongariro is the right river.

Non-fishing guests don't need to engage with this at all. The lodge works equally well as a place to simply be.

## The dining

The dining room serves a menu that changes daily, built around local produce and the rhythms of the Central Plateau seasons. Dinner is typically four courses. The wine list is serious, with an exceptional selection of Central Otago pinot noirs alongside the broader New Zealand and international offering.

Breakfast on the terrace above the river, when the morning is clear, is one of the more quietly remarkable starts to a day in New Zealand.

## How we build it into a journey

We most often position Huka Lodge as the second or third property in a North Island itinerary — after Kauri Cliffs in Northland, or as the first major stop after an Auckland arrival. From Taupō, the Tongariro Crossing is accessible (one of New Zealand's most dramatic day walks), as is the Wairarapa wine country and Wellington.

Two nights is the minimum to feel the pace of the place. Three nights, if the itinerary allows, lets you go fishing, do the Crossing, and still have a day where you do nothing at all — which, in our experience, is often the day guests remember most.
