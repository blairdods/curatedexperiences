The Michelin Key system for hotels and lodges — launched globally as an extension of the MICHELIN Guide's culinary programme — has recognised a substantial number of New Zealand properties. The list reflects what careful travellers have known for some time: that the country's lodge sector is among the finest in the world.

What follows is the full current listing, organised by Key level and region.

## Two Keys: exceptional stays

Two-Key properties are those Michelin deems exceptional destinations in their own right — properties defined by genuine character, architectural ambition, and world-class service within an authentically New Zealand setting.

**Huka Lodge, Taupō** — The original benchmark of New Zealand lodge luxury, operating on the banks of the Waikato River since 1924. The standard against which everything else is measured.

**The Lindis, Ahuriri Valley** — A futuristic nature lodge in the remote South Island high country, famous for its low-profile architecture that dissolves into the tawny grasslands of the Ahuriri Valley.

**Rosewood Cape Kidnappers, Hawke's Bay** — A cliffside retreat set on an 800-acre working farm above the Pacific. The Tom Doak golf course, the gannet colony at the point, and the farm-to-table dining make it a property with genuine range.

**Rosewood Kauri Cliffs, Northland** — Panoramic ocean views over the Bay of Islands, a par-72 course running along clifftop headlands, and the quiet authority of a property that has had twenty-five years to become what it is.

**Blanket Bay, Glenorchy** — The quintessential alpine-wilderness lodge on the shores of Lake Wakatipu, close enough to Queenstown to be accessible, remote enough to feel genuinely away.

**Otahuna Lodge, near Christchurch** — A meticulously restored Victorian mansion on the Canterbury Plains, famed for its historic kitchen gardens and the quality of the estate dining they produce.

**Wharekauhau Country Estate, Wairarapa** — An Edwardian country house on a 5,500-acre sheep station, with the Palliser Bay coastline and its wildlife below.

## One Key: very special stays

One-Key properties deliver distinct personality and service that goes beyond the functional — each is notable for something specific.

### Auckland and surrounds

**The Hotel Britomart** — New Zealand's first 5-Green Star hotel, in the CBD's heritage precinct. The eco-conscious design and the quality of the surrounding neighbourhood dining make it the best urban stay in the country.

**Park Hyatt Auckland** — Premium waterfront position in Wynyard Quarter, with harbour views and room scale that few Auckland properties can match.

**Hotel Fitzroy curated by Fable, Ponsonby** — A moody, considered boutique stay in Auckland's best inner suburb. The right choice for guests who want character over scale.

**Delamore Lodge, Waiheke Island** — Perched on the cliffs above the Hauraki Gulf, with a view back to the city skyline that changes entirely with the light.

### Queenstown and Central Otago

**Eichardt's Private Hotel** — The lakefront address in the centre of Queenstown, with the history and the view to justify its position.

**Rosewood Matakauri** — Design-forward lake and mountain luxury looking directly at the Remarkables. Among the most visually arresting hotel rooms in the country.

**Azur Lodge** — Freestanding villas on a hillside above Lake Wakatipu, each with uninterrupted floor-to-ceiling views. Quiet and private in a way that's difficult to achieve in Queenstown.

**Stoneridge Estate, Lake Hayes** — Boutique vineyard lodging with striking stone architecture and the gentle pace of a property that doesn't need to try very hard.

**Gibbston Valley Lodge and Spa** — Fully integrated into the winemaking landscape of the gorge, with cellar access and the deep pinot noir country surrounding it.

### Regional New Zealand

**Eagles Nest, Bay of Islands** — Multi-award-winning private villas high on a ridgeline above Russell, with views across the Bay that justify the position entirely.

**Solitaire Lodge, Lake Tarawera** — A peaceful lakeside lodge in the heart of Rotorua's geothermal and cultural landscape, with a quality of stillness that's rare in the region.

**Bay of Many Coves, Marlborough Sounds** — A remote coastal retreat accessible only by boat or helicopter, which is both the inconvenience and the point.

## How we use this

The Michelin Key is one signal among several we use when designing a journey. The properties on this list are all ones we know well — some we've been working with for years, others have come into clearer focus as the Key system has brought them wider attention.

If you're deciding between two properties in the same region, the Key level is a useful indicator of how the experience is likely to feel. But the more useful conversation is about which properties match your particular interests: whether that's a property with exceptional fishing, one with serious wine access, or one that's primarily about the landscape.
