Source: traveltrade.newzealand.com (Tourism New Zealand's English-language global travel-trade portal). Pages fetched and extracted: homepage, /find-your-100-percent/, /tools-and-resources/downloadable-resources/, plus deep links to itinerary, regional and themed PDFs.

## What the trade site is

Tourism New Zealand (TNZ) describes the trade portal in its own words:

> *"Welcome to our travel trade site providing destination training, tools and resources to help you confidently sell Aotearoa New Zealand holidays."*

It is a single-stop hub that gives travel agents and tour operators (a) **destination training** (the NZ Specialist Programme), (b) **selling tools** (itineraries, maps, brand assets, infographics, factsheets), (c) **familiarisation programmes** (self-famils via the Explore NZ Discount Programme; sponsored famils via TNZ events), and (d) **commercial campaigns** to plug into (100% Pure New Zealand, Active Escapes, Worth a Special Journey).

**The four primary trade-site tasks the homepage points agents toward:**
1. Become a New Zealand expert → 100% Pure New Zealand Specialist Programme
2. Plan your New Zealand self-famil → Explore New Zealand Discount Programme
3. Discover what's new in New Zealand → /news/
4. Download New Zealand itineraries → /tools-and-resources/downloadable-resources/

Localised versions of the site exist for the UK (`/uk/`), Australia (`/au/`), USA (`/us/`), Germany (`/de/`), China (`/cn/`), India (`/in/`), Korea (`/kr/`), Singapore (`/se/`) and Japan (`/jp/`). Use the right localised version when serving clients from those markets.

## Practical info to anchor every NZ pitch

Pulled from the trade site's stated positioning and the canonical agent handouts:

- **Both islands matter, but they're different sells.** North Island = culture, geothermal, beaches, food/wine, cities. South Island = mountains, lakes, Great Walks, fjords, ski. Most premium itineraries combine both.
- **Year-round destination.** TNZ's current campaign positioning is explicitly that NZ is iconic year-round, not just summer. Lean into shoulder/off-peak (March–November) — see the winter weather infographic and the "Spring in Aotearoa" / "Autumn in Aotearoa" theme guides for talking points.
- **NZeTA + Visitor Visa.** Many travellers need an NZeTA (electronic travel authority); some need a visitor visa. The trade-site Entry Requirements PDF is the agent's source-of-truth.
- **MICHELIN Guide 2026.** New news hook for 2026 — NZ now appears in the MICHELIN Guide. Use this in food-and-wine pitches.
- **Māori culture is a core selling pillar.** TNZ frames the destination as *"the powerful connection between people, place and culture"* — Māori cultural experiences (Rotorua, Northland, Manea Footprints of Kupe, Te Papa) are not an add-on, they are part of the headline brand promise.
- **English is universal**, currency is the New Zealand dollar (NZD), drives on the left, no tipping culture (but service workers appreciate it for exceptional service). Power: 230V / Type I plug.

# 2. 100% Pure New Zealand Specialist Programme

## 1. What the programme is

The **100% Pure New Zealand Specialist Programme** is Tourism New Zealand's official online destination-training programme for travel agents, advisors, wholesalers and product managers. The site describes it as:

> "Our 100% Pure New Zealand Specialist Programme is a fun and interactive way to increase your knowledge of New Zealand. Get inspired, increase your sales and help create your clients' dream trip."

And in the body of the landing page:

> "Introducing our refreshed, insight-driven online training programme. With new features like shorter, simplified courses and the ability to complete them on any device, learning has never been more convenient."

Headline framing on the page: **"Training that leads to travel"** and **"Become a destination expert and unlock your self-famil adventure"**.

### Who runs it
- **Tourism New Zealand** (the Tourism NZ Travel Trade division). The corporate site is `tourismnewzealand.com`; the trade-facing site is `traveltrade.newzealand.com`. The learning platform itself is delivered on Totara (a Moodle-derived LMS) at `onlinemodules.newzealand.com`. The login footer notes: *"Powered by Totara Talent Experience Platform."*

### Who it's for
- Travel sellers worldwide. The Explore eligibility section spells the audience out: *"Work full-time as a travel seller - advisor, wholesaler, or product manager."*
- The site is localised for these markets (each with its own URL prefix): International English (default), United Kingdom (`/uk/`), Australia (`/au/`), United States (`/us/`), Germany (`/de/`), China (`/cn/`), India (`/in/`), Korea (`/kr/`), Southeast Asia / Singapore (`/se/`), Japan (`/jp/`).

### Cost
- **No cost is stated anywhere.** All sign-up CTAs lead directly to free account creation on the Totara LMS — there is no payment step, no fee mentioned in the FAQs, and no pricing language in the marketing copy. The programme is positioned as a free Tourism NZ industry benefit.

### Languages supported
The login page (`onlinemodules.newzealand.com/login/index.php`) explicitly offers the LMS interface in five languages:
- Deutsch (Neu) — `de`
- English — `en` (default)
- 한국어 (Korean) — `ko`
- 日本語 (Japanese) — `ja`
- 繁體中文 (Traditional Chinese) — `zh_tw`

The marketing site additionally serves country-specific English variants (UK / AU / US / IN / SE).

## 2. Programme structure: levels, courses, time, certification

The page section **"How does the training programme work?"** states verbatim:

> "There are two specialist levels: **Graduate** and **Advanced**. To achieve each level, the requirements are:"

### Graduate Specialist
> "**Complete eight foundation courses**, each course takes approximately 25 minutes from start to finish.
>
> Once you become a Graduate Specialist, you will be eligible to apply for our Explore New Zealand Discount Programme and curate your own ultimate self-famil for you and a travel companion."

FAQ confirms: *"This status does not have an expiry date."*

### Advanced Specialist
> "**Complete any four advanced courses** and **provide proof that you've visited Aotearoa New Zealand**. To maintain your Advanced Specialist status, you'll need to complete a further two advanced courses every two years. Proof of your trip to New Zealand does not expire."

The FAQ adds: *"Advanced Specialists are required to complete any two advanced courses every two years to keep their Advanced Specialist status. Your expiry date is shown on your personal dashboard and we'll send you an email reminder closer to your expiry date. Please note that your New Zealand trip evidence does not expire — you only need to visit Aotearoa New Zealand once to retain your Advanced Specialist status."*

### Time required
- **~25 minutes per course** (each foundation course is "approximately 25 minutes")
- Graduate Specialist (8 foundation courses) ≈ 200 minutes total of learning
- Advanced Specialist adds ≥ 4 advanced courses (≈ 100+ extra minutes) plus visit-proof
- Maintenance: 2 advanced courses every 2 years

### Skipping courses
> "Consider yourself a New Zealand expert? Pass the knowledge tests to skip learning modules" — i.e. courses contain knowledge tests that can short-circuit the lesson content if the learner already knows the material.

### Legacy tier mapping (from FAQs)
The new two-tier structure replaces an older Bronze/Silver/Gold scheme. From the FAQ verbatim:
- "Yes, **Bronze Specialists are transferred to Graduate Specialists** in the new programme. You can still access the Foundation Courses to gain essential New Zealand knowledge, plus you have access to Advanced Courses to gain specialist knowledge."
- "Yes, **Silver Specialists are transferred to Graduate Specialists** in the new programme..."
- "Yes, **Gold Specialists are transferred to Graduate Specialists** in the new programme. Your New Zealand trip evidence has been transferred to your personal dashboard. To reach Advanced Specialist, simply complete four advanced courses."

Existing Bronze/Silver/Gold accounts retain their profile, access and login.

> *"New Zealand's off-peak seasons appeal to visitors from India."*

**Headline stats**
- 81,000 Indian visitors (YE May 2025); **30% holiday share** (lowest holiday share — VFR/business is significant).
- Average length of stay: **11 days** (holiday).
- **71% off-peak arrivals** — the highest off-peak skew of any market.
- Total spend NZ$266.1 million (NZ$67.4 million from holidaymakers); **average trip NZ$5,335 / NZ$5,402 holiday**.
- Active Considerers: **27 million across New Delhi, Bangalore and Mumbai**; **63%** rank NZ as their top preference destination.

**What Indian visitors want to do**
Natural attractions, walks and hikes, geothermal parks. Motivations: beautiful landscapes and scenery, having fun, getting in touch with nature.

**Sales angle**
India is TNZ's biggest off-peak opportunity — the 71% off-peak share is structural, not coincidental, and the AC pool (27 million in three Tier-1 cities) is large and high-intent (63% rank NZ #1). Itineraries should combine geothermal/cultural (Rotorua, Taupō) with Mount Cook / Lake Tekapo and Milford. Vegetarian/Indian dietary support across operators is a practical sales talking point.

## Singapore

Singapore has a **2025 Market Snapshot** (Singapore-v5.pdf) but is not yet broken out on TNZ's main markets-overview page with the same level of public detail as the other seven markets.

**What's published:**
- TNZ flags Singapore as a **key target market** with airline connectivity from Auckland and a high-spending, English-speaking, easy-to-service traveller base.
- Singapore is grouped with India and South Korea in the H1 FY26 **Active Considerer report bundle** for "Asia (India + South Korea + Singapore)" — a useful supplementary data source for agents working this market.

**Sales angle (general)**
Treat Singapore similarly to high-yield Asian short-stay markets: short itineraries (4–10 days) with high accommodation and dining standards, scenic hero experiences, and pre-bookable premium add-ons (helicopter tours, scenic cruises, luxury lodges). The Singapore Market Snapshot PDF is the canonical detailed source — see "Source URLs" below.

## How to read regional dispersal across markets

A pattern emerges in the data that should shape itinerary design:

- **Multi-region, long-stay markets:** UK (55% visit 4+ regions, 15 days), Germany (62% visit 4+ regions, 16 days). Build 2–3 week, 4+ region itineraries.
- **Two-island short-stay markets:** Japan (37% visit both islands but average only 8 days). Build tight 7–10 day "highlights" itineraries.
- **Concentrated or repeat markets:** Australia (9 days, frequent travellers, often single-region); China (9 days, high preference, hero scenery focus). Single-island itineraries work well.
- **Off-peak heavy markets:** India (71%), Australia (69%), China (68%), Japan (64%). Lead with March–November storytelling.
- **Premium yield markets:** Germany (NZ$8,648 holiday spend), UK (NZ$7,284 holiday spend), USA (NZ$6,360). Premium lodges, guided Great Walks, helicopter scenic flights.
