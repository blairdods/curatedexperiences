## Voice Note 1 — 23 Apr, 9:14pm (11 min)
**Topic: Gary Duffy / Discover Holidays best practice — Sales & Operations**

### Client Acquisition — Direct vs Trade
- Clients come to the "shop front" (website) because they want to speak to an **expert with genuine local knowledge** who has visited the destination themselves
- **Authenticity and credibility** are core factors
- Avoid leaning too hard on travel agencies — direct-to-consumer is the core aim
- Direct consumers are very engaged and very knowledgeable
- Challenge: competing with direct lodge bookings — the answer is the **curated service a lodge can't replicate**
- Key: providing a personalised curator the client can have an ongoing relationship with

### American Client Expectations
- HNW clients want **service, service, service** — attentiveness is non-negotiable
- They're personal and may ask personal questions — lean into this, it builds trust
- Be human — connect with someone who has genuinely experienced what's being sold
- **Credibility is essential**

### Itinerary Design
- Itineraries are **templated at the core, then customised** based on individual preferences (accommodation differences, special requirements)
- Most clients simply want to visit NZ and don't arrive with deep knowledge — they rely on CE's experience
- **Freedom of choice is highly valued** — offering activity options each day makes clients feel in control
- **Modules are a powerful structural tool** — reusable itinerary segments that slot across multiple trips; efficient, easy to maintain, proven quality

### What Clients Value & Will Pay Premium For
- **Knowledge and service above all else**
- Expert advice — feel guided by someone who genuinely knows
- After-sales contact — signals care, builds loyalty
- **Mid-trip check-in** — goes a long way and is remembered

### Team Structure
- If appropriately resourced: **sales should stay focused on sales**, separate from on-ground support
- Dedicated emergency line
- **Travel designers** = main contact throughout trip, driven by genuine interest (not obligation)
- Travel designers should be **incentivised**

### Conversion & Sales Process
- Industry inquiry-to-booking target: **~50%**
- **Phone calls strongly prioritised** — far easier to build trust, connect personally, and close
- Must have coverage across different time zones
- Email = fallback, not primary channel
- AI bot should offer ability to **defer to a human** — "You've called during NZ office hours, would you like to connect with someone?"

### Key Priorities
- Contracting, structures, costing sheets, templating
- Training, getting competitive net rates
- Website must be **usable and friendly** — people need to understand it and be trained on it

### Inquiry Drop-off
- Biggest reason for drop-off: failure to establish trust early, slow response times, over-reliance on email
- **1.52 seconds to engage** on website, then how it develops from there

### Repeat Business & Referrals
- NZ trip often once-in-a-lifetime/bucket list — repeat unlikely but **referrals are key**
- Post-program surveys — optimal way to get feedback, hear their voice
- Lead times: ~4 months inquiry to program, sometimes 6-12 months
