# Curated Experiences — US Google Ads PPC Plan

*July 2026 | Targeting VHNWI/UHNWI Americans, age 40-60, $25K–$80K USD per booking*

---

## 1. Strategic Framing

### The brief in one sentence
Get in front of high-income Americans who are actively planning a high-end international trip — before they find a competitor — and route them into the AI concierge, which does the qualification and conversion work.

### What PPC is doing here
Tony's insight is that the website is a **validation and conversion tool, not a discovery tool** in year one. PPC accelerates discovery. Once someone lands, the concierge takes over. The job of every ad is narrow: earn the click from the right person. The job of the landing page is narrower still: get them into the chat.

### Why Google Search (not Meta first)
These clients don't browse Instagram dreaming of NZ — they search when they're ready to plan. Search intent is the highest purchase signal available. Meta retargeting comes in Phase 2 once there's a pixel audience to build from.

## 6. Ad Copy Guidelines

### Structural Rules for All RSAs

- **Headline 1**: Anchor — who we are or what we offer
- **Headline 2**: Differentiator — what makes this different
- **Headline 3**: CTA or proof point
- **Description 1**: Emotional/narrative — paint the experience
- **Description 2**: Rational — credentials, urgency, or next step

### Voice Rules (from brand guide)

- Never use: tour, package, agent, amazing, awesome, book now, limited time, hidden gem, bucket list
- Always use: journey, experience, curated, private, bespoke, personalised, expert
- Tone: warm, unhurried, confident — not corporate or salesy
- Never use a price unless it's a floor (e.g., "from $25,000 USD per couple") — and only where relevant
- One CTA direction per ad: either "chat with our concierge" or "start planning" — never both

### Ad Extensions (Assets) — All Campaigns

| Asset | Content |
|---|---|
| **Sitelinks** | Our Journeys / The Masterpiece / Fiordland / Chat with Concierge |
| **Callouts** | Award-Winning NZ Operator · Private & Personalised · 24/7 Concierge · World Travel Awards |
| **Structured Snippets** | Destinations: Fiordland, Queenstown, Marlborough, Bay of Islands, Rotorua, Kaikōura |
| **Image assets** | Milford Sound aerial, Kauri Cliffs golf, Marlborough vineyard, luxury lodge arrival |
| **Lead form** | Name + Email + "Tell us about your dream NZ journey" — fires as lead in CRM |
| **Call extension** | Phase 2 only (once Tony/Liam have dedicated line set up) |

## 7. Landing Page Strategy

### The Golden Rule

Every ad group should land on the most contextually relevant page — not the homepage. A visitor who searched "luxury Fiordland tour" and lands on the homepage has to do work. That's friction, and friction kills conversion.

| Campaign | Destination Page |
|---|---|
| Brand | Homepage (`/`) |
| Competitor | Homepage or `/about` (emphasise differentiation) |
| Luxury NZ generic | Homepage with concierge pre-opened |
| South Island — Fiordland | `/destinations/fiordland` |
| South Island — Queenstown | `/destinations/queenstown` |
| South Island — Marlborough | `/destinations/marlborough` |
| North Island — Bay of Islands | `/destinations/northland` |
| North Island — Rotorua | `/destinations/rotorua` |
| Food & wine | `/journeys/the-epicurean` |
| Wildlife/conservation | `/destinations/kaikoura` or `/journeys/the-discovery` |
| Heli experiences | `/journeys/the-expedition` |

### Concierge Handoff from Ads

The UTM source classification is already wired (`source = google_ads` → `cta_tone = direct`, `concierge_greeting = high-intent`). This means a visitor from a Google Ad automatically gets:

- More direct hero CTA copy ("Start Planning Your Journey")
- A concierge opening line tuned for someone who searched with intent, not curiosity

This is a genuine conversion advantage — make sure the concierge system prompt reflects that Google Ads visitors already know what they want; skip the soft exploration stage.

### Landing Page CRO Checklist

For each destination/journey page serving as a PPC landing page, verify:

- [ ] Hero video loads within 2 seconds on desktop
- [ ] Concierge button is visible above the fold without scrolling
- [ ] Page has a clear single CTA — not three competing options
- [ ] Trust signals visible on first scroll: PPG Tours + World Travel Awards mention
- [ ] No navigation to `/admin`, login, or irrelevant internal pages indexed by Google
- [ ] Schema markup present (`TouristDestination`, `TravelAction`)
- [ ] Page load Core Web Vitals pass (LCP < 2.5s, CLS < 0.1)

## 10. Optimisation Cadence

### Weekly (every Monday — SEO+Ads agent can surface this)

- Review search term reports — add negatives
- Check impression share lost to budget vs. rank
- Review CTR by ad — pause underperformers below 2% CTR after 200 impressions
- Flag any anomalies in cost spikes or conversion drops
- Review geographic performance by state

### Monthly

- Rotate in new RSA headline/description variants for any ad group below 4/5 Ad Strength
- Review audience observation data — identify segments with CPL 20%+ below average and increase bids
- Review device performance — adjust mobile bid modifier
- Review dayparting data — refine time-of-day bid adjustments
- Generate monthly report: spend, impressions, clicks, CTR, CPC, leads, CPL, ROAS

### 90-Day Review

- Pause any campaign with CPL > 3× the average
- Identify the single best-performing campaign and increase its budget by 50%
- Review competitor keyword landscape — new entrants?
- Assess Quality Scores on core keywords — low QS (<5) means landing page or relevance issue
- Decide whether to launch Performance Max

### Key Metrics & Targets

| Metric | Phase 1 Target | Phase 2 Target |
|---|---|---|
| CTR (Search) | >3% | >5% |
| CPC average | <$8 NZD | <$6 NZD |
| Cost per lead | <$300 NZD | <$200 NZD |
| Lead-to-proposal rate | 30%+ | 50% (Tony's target) |
| ROAS | Track only | >5× by Month 6 |

## 11. A/B Testing Roadmap

### Month 1–2 — Ad Copy Tests

Run 2 RSA variants per ad group and let Google rotate. After 300 impressions per variant, pin the winner's headlines in the winning positions and test a new challenger.

**Test 1**: Emotional hook vs. proof/credibility headline
**Test 2**: "Concierge" CTA vs. "Start Planning" CTA
**Test 3**: Price floor in headline ("from $25,000 USD") vs. no price

### Month 3–4 — Landing Page Tests

**Test 1**: Homepage with concierge auto-opened vs. homepage standard
**Test 2**: Journey page vs. destination page for Fiordland ad group
**Test 3**: Lead form asset (on-SERP) vs. landing page conversion

### Month 5–6 — Audience Tests

**Test 1**: Top 10% income only vs. Top 10–20% at reduced bid
**Test 2**: Life events audience (anniversary/retirement) uplift test
**Test 3**: Lookalike from converted leads vs. custom intent audience
